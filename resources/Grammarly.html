A perspective that I have developed over the years, in the context of a purposeful life, is that one should dedicate oneself to the creation of tangible value to society and the economy. As another tenet, I strongly believe that maximal individual contribution can be achieved through entrepreneurship. An entrepreneur creates value at two levels: at a primary level, through the creation of products that society can utilize and at a secondary level, through the creation of jobs. It is also important to note that the value-creation scales with the innovative nature of the product. In this sense, a more innovative product is a more valuable product. With this perspective, I delved into the field of robotics as it seemed like a conjunction of three core fields Computer Science and Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, and Electrical Engineering. With the knowledge of the three disciplines, one is uniquely placed in terms of problem-solving skills to tackle the most challenging issues of the world. Innovative solutions to these problems would be of immense value to society, and I hope to achieve something of this sort in my life as well. As a starting point in this journey, I hope to learn sufficient business and technical skills at the Masters in Robotics System Development Programme (MRSD) at CMU, which will later help me contribute to society in a very real sense.

I was introduced to robotics at the very beginning of my undergraduate years as a member of Team Humanoid, a body of undergraduates meant to participate in international competitions such as FIRA HUROCUP. While the team had participated in 2019 and had won awards, we couldnt participate in the next year due to COVID-19. With the lockdown, these student activities halted for a while. With no scope for hardware development because of the pandemic, I engaged in the software aspect of the team. I helped my team create navigation and localization modules. Eventually, I became the head of the team as COVID-19 subsided. At that time, the teams funds had emptied because of no renewal during the COVID-19 pandemic, and the senior members had left the institute. Aiming to revive the team, I decided we must play to our strengths and systematically address our weaknesses over time. We targeted the ICRA 2023 Simulated Humanoid Wrestling Competition, which involved only software. We eventually placed in the top 32 globally, and in another rendition of the same competition, the IROS 2023 Simulated Humanoid Wrestling Competition, we placed in the top 16 globally. Right now, the team is thriving with sufficient funds and is planning a complete hardware overhaul of our robot.

Motivated to explore more, I took institute courses relevant to robotics: Introduction to Robotics, Robotics: Manipulator and Control, Convex Optimization, Introduction to Machine Learning, Basic Modern Control System and Dynamics, obtaining the highest grade in all but two of these. While I had chosen these specifically for their relevance to robotics, I had also done a lot of other courses that were part of my degree program template, namely Design of Machine Elements, Vibrations and Controls, Introduction to Electronics, Real Analysis, Linear Algebra, Mechanisms and Machinery. With such a spectrum of subjects, I was well-equipped to handle the complexities of robotics, both in the software and hardware. In the penultimate and ultimate years of my degree, I took up reading and implementing more complex ideas such as Null-space projected Inverse Dynamics Controllers, Gaussian Processes, Contact Models, Rigid Body Algorithms and Reinforcement Learning.

My motivation to pursue MRSD primarily comes from the experience I had in research group in IIT Kanpur Mobile Robotics Laboratory. The lab was building a quadruped robot akin in capability to the ones made by Boston Dynamics, and ETHZ. The lab made a strong impression on me in how much craftsmanship goes into creating an excellent product. In this lab I was surrounded by much more experienced and capable people. I learnt through them the rigor that should go into creating a product. I assisted them by building the contact models for the simulation of the robot where I leveraged my knowledge of convex optimization to solve a Linear Complementarity Problem (LCP) describing the contact using projected Gauss Seidel Method. Later I delved into Gaussian Process assisted state estimation module where we sought to estimate the uncertainty in our guessed values of the parameters such as the Mass matrix of the robot, etc. Later I was entrusted to kickstart the labs endeavor into reinforcement learning, brainstorming five RL projects and planning them out over months. This was especially hard as I had no background in RL and had to learn everything in 2 months. It was a difficult but rewarding experience to learn a complex field on my own, but I accomplished it by breaking it down into manageable sections.

My experience in the lab led me to realize that research and business are quite similar. Both delve into the unknown. Business involves creation of products out of disjointed systems and research involves creation of something unique out of seemingly unrelated concepts. What I have taken away from this realization is that both disciplines demand a high level of thoroughness, dedication and creativity.

The program that I am enrolled at Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur is an integrated Bachelor-masters degree majoring in Mechanical Engineering. While the program already contains a masters, my rationale for applying in another masters primarily stems on the opportunities that I will get. I feel that obtaining a masters degree specializing in robotics from CMU will open doors to various professional avenues involved in cutting-edge technology.

My perspective aligns almost perfectly with the aim of the MRSD program at CMU. The MRSD program is unique in the combined study of robotics technology and business. As I understand, the Tepper School of Business conducts the business courses, and the Robotics Institute conducts the technical courses. When I encountered this program for the first time a couple years ago, it struck me in how well the program suited me. Its business-oriented courses would help me realize my ambition of opening a business, and its much-reputed Robotics Institute, will guide me to the innovation that I so highly value. I feel I am ideally suited for this program. Since the program structure aligns so closely with my professional objectives, I am poised to be a dedicated and enthusiastic student at CMU, intent on contributing positively to reputation of the university.
